    Christopher Allen presented on "Edge Identifiers & Clique... 2025-01-15 tang_talks
    Christopher Allen presented on "Edge Identifiers & Cliques" at W3C CCG. Christopher started with a brief history on cryptography, the traditional single-signature paradigm, and the pitfalls of such single-signature paradigm. #Identity is about relationships, so he proposes “relational edge identity” using Schnorr signature to model these identity relationships. Instead of a public key representing an entity, we would have a cryptographic clique identifier (i.e. a group public key) that represents a group of relationship edges composing the identity. Such clique identifiers empower decentralized identity management, peer-based identity authentication, SPOC/SPOF resilience, secret group decision making, and enhanced privacy. This is a must-watch session, and you can watch the recording of the presentation and discussion here (start at 15 min 20 sec mark): https://t.co/OA4V95ey7N
