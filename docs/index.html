    This picture is probably one of the earliest examples of ... 2024-04-26 ankurb
    This picture is probably one of the earliest examples of a "deepfake": known as the Cottingley Fairies picture, it's one of a series of pictures taken by two kids. It gained notoriety after Arthur Conan Doyle (yes, the creator of Sherlock) gave them credibility as "real evidence" of the existence of fairies. As generative AI image/video/audio becomes more popular, more life-like, and cheaper to produce, we're in for a reckoning on what we can trust that's shared online. Especially in a year when 65+ countries around the world are holding elections. I'm almost certain that deepfakes (unfortunately) are going to sway at least one of these elections, and people will really start paying attention to what's human-generated vs not. So while the decentralized identity space has traditionally focussed a lot on making digital replicas of paper/plastic documents, making KYC easier etc, IMO one of the sleeping giants of use cases that will accelerate the adoption of decentralised identity is the concept of "Content Credentials", which provide a chain-of-custody on what edits happened to image, and whether it's AI-generated or not. Editing in itself is not a "bad thing", since there are a lot of legitimate reasons to edit a photo (maybe you want to crop it, or fix blemishes). And whether an audio-visual is AI-generated or not is not binary, since you could have a real actor on an AI-generated background. Read more to dive deeper into how @cheqd_io's tech - among many other interoperable industry solutions. -can be applied to this emerging space of Content Credentials 👇🏾
