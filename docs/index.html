    Without standardization, identities and credentials issue... 2024-10-09 PrivadoID
    Without standardization, identities and credentials issued by different organizations may not work seamlessly across apps and platforms, leading to fragmentation and inefficiencies Privado ID addresses this challenge by following W3C standards to ensure global interoperability of decentralized identity systems: - Decentralized Identifiers (DIDs): Let users create unique identifiers without permission from any authority –you can create them locally in your wallet, much like how you can create an Ethereum address - Verifiable Credentials (VCs): Enable the private storage and content sharing of attributes about an identity (e.g. your government might issue you a verifiable credential that proves your date of birth). These credentials are stored in a standard format so that any verifier can read and understand it. These two standards—DIDs and VCs—are key to how Privado ID operates to ensure interoperability across different platforms and apps
